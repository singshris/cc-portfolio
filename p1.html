Lost Object

Link to code

Description

My partner Rama Deshpande described her lost object as so:
I lost a soft toy that I had won in a claw machine on my first ever solo trip to Japan. It was a small mouse-like creature that was white in color. It had small round brown eyes and a round brown nose. Its ears were semi-circular and pink. It was also wearing a shirt with a white collar and white and pink stripes across it. There were two tiny hands and feet. It had a small chain to attach a key-ring to it or hang it from somewhere.

Design Process

I already had a strong foundation to work off of. Rama was very concise and clear with her description, giving me several parameters--enough to create but not overburden. My initial interpretations tackled the object from two views: cross-section and top. The top-view from the outset seemed a better composition that displayed all of Rama's mentioned elements with ease. Halfway through my first-ever JavaScript coding however, the decision did feel hasty because the object looked nothing like how I'd envisioned. I'm glad I stuck with it though, because toward the end, it shaped up much better that I thought it would.

Reflection

A key realization was that of the importance of organization. It may be an assumption taken for granted but as we lay down one line of code after another, it gets that much harder to keep track of the end-product especially when it involves "layers". My color palette was where I was able to insert my own history; that of a designer who favors the soft and pastel hues. All in all, I am pleased with my first attempt at illustrating through coding, and I only hope to get better from this point on.
