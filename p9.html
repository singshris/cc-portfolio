Your face as pixels but with consent

For some reason, the embed doesn't load one of the buttons. If possible, please view the sketch in the editor for the complete experience.

Description

I used buttons to strike up a series of 3 scenes that lead the viewer to experience filters in addition to the ones introduced in experimental camera. However, instead of simply bombarding the user with the feed as soon as the sketch loads, I now include a series of instructions about what's happening and what the sketch will be demanding from the user i.e. consent to start their webcam. The intention of the sketch has been updated from being a self-referential warning about present-day surveillance into becoming a repository of fun "filters" that are mapped to the viewers face.

Process

I sketched out a simple flowchart to map out how someone can be guided through each scene.

  • 1. The switch statement was employed to load 3 scenes: a) To welcome the viewer into the sketch, b) to inform them that their consent is needed to proceed, c) to give them the option to leave at any point.
  • 2. You also get the option to save whatever camera filter you like. The saved images, at this stage, include everything on the canvas but I'm working to find a way to save only the face without the DOM elements or text instructions.
  • 3. The goal is to keep creating new filters built from tinier elements. As you can see, a fourth image featuring the wave from The Great Wave off Kanagawa by Hokusai.

    • Reflection

  • The sketch attempts to address the F.R.I.E.S model in the following ways:
  • a. Freely-given: The camera does not start until a user allows them permission to do so using the "Yes" or "No buttons".
  • b. Reversible: The "Leave" button is designed to get someone of the scene that actively utilizes the camera. At this point, a challenge is to actually stop the webcam everyone someone leaves instead of just switching scenes.
  • c. Informed: The copy included in this iteration of experimental camera tries to keep the user as informed as possible about what to expect of their experience.
  • d. Enthusiastic: No additional inputs are demanded of the user if they want to access the face filters.
  • e. Specific: Because the functionality to stop createCapture once the user exits the corresponding scene involves out-of-syllabus functions like tracks and callbacks, it still isn't perfect in being specific. The user exits the scene but the camera continues to show live feed--the solutions to which are presently being investigated.
