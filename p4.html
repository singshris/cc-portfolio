Exquisite Corpse

Link to code

Description

This project incorporated the sketches made by my partners Lila Feldman & Zhenyu Yang.



















My Sketch

In my own turn, I concocted a triangle-candycane-strawberry non-human otherwordly being.

Design Process

1. I built a composite body that borrowed the clean and simple strokes from Lila's moody man, the color-changing properties of Zhenyu's friendly alien, and the playfulness of my candy cane strawberry creature.

2. The corpse's final form interprets certain traits from individual sketches slightly differently in order to homogenize its physical attributes. For instance, since Zhenyu and I chose to stick to uniform straight edges, I straightened out the curves in Lila's sketch to fit the style. Originally, thought out as a bright red-faced candy-esque manifestation, my non-human being adjusted to the dynamic color scheme so the head and torso were in unison.

3. The legs were maintained in greyscale to honor the outline-structure of its original sketch


Reflection

  • 1. Incorporating user-defined functions significantly helped structure my code (it still has a long way to go in terms of brevity and eliminating redundancies).
  • 2. Solely based on the curves in Lila's sketch, I wish I'd learned the Bezier function but using straight lines worked better for the overall esthetic so I leave it be. A different iteration could perhaps employ Bezier curves just for practise.
  • 3. The greatest challenge that I overcame to some extent was experimenting with the For loop. Instead of marking each shape's co-ordinates I used the for loop to execute the geometrical pattern of the torso portion, however, success with the logic ended up taking nearly as much time as plotting the co-ordinates manually.
